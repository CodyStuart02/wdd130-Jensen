Memories from Madrid South
Jan 2023 - Feb 2025

I was serving in Ciudad Real with one of my favorite companions, Elder Hoffman, and we had a crazy experience. As a preface, in Spain missionaries go to bed at 11:30 instead of 10:30 like most missions do it. It was about 11:45 and we were both in bed trying to sleep when we got a really bad feeling. We got up and searched the apartment to find out what could be wrong. After 15 minutes of thinking and praying for guidance, as clear as day we both felt the impression to leave immediately. We got our emergency backpacks, left our apartment to go to the church building and called our district leader. We ended up spending the night in the chapel and we slept on some chairs we pulled together. The next morning, upon returning to the apartment, we found damage on the door, right next to the frame where the lock was. Someone had tried to break in the night before. I don’t know what would have happened had we stayed, but what I do know is that God knew that would happened and warned us to leave. I know the Spirit speaks to us to protect us and guide us, and I will never disregard a spiritual impression.

I spent two transfers of my mission in a coastal city called Alicante. It was a fairly big city with eight missionaries assigned to the two wards there, and it was a beautiful place. Still to this day one of my favorite places I’ve visited. In this area came my favorite mission experience. We received a referral from social media of a man by the name of Jader. He expressed interest in the gospel, and we reached out to him, to find out that he was deaf. So we set up an appointment with him and invited some missionaries from Seattle who were assigned to an ASL speaking mission. Through the process, I started learning ASL to be able to have basic conversation with him, and it was one of the most special things I’ve ever done. The gospel completely changed his life. He loves the Book of Mormon, and he loves Jesus Christ. He would travel from his home to the church, an hour and a half each way three times a week for sacrament meetings and lessons, and continued to devour the scriptures and learn more about the doctrines of the kingdom. When the time came, I learned the baptismal prayer in ASL and baptized him for the remission of sins. He and I have a forever bond, and we always will. Later on, the stars aligned for me to go with him to his first temple trip, and I baptized him in the temple for one of his deceased ancestors. The gospel of Jesus Christ is nothing short of a miracle, and the change it makes in peoples’ is indescribable.
